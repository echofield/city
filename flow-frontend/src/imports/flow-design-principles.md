1. The Map Should Behave Like a Radar Field

Right now your map shows:

districts lighting up

event anchors

signal panel on the right

That’s good.

But to make it feel like a real city radar, the map should emphasize energy zones, not just geography.

Instead of:

arrondissement shapes glowing

Think more like:

soft demand fields

Example concept:

weak demand → faint glow
medium demand → larger glow
strong demand → bright core

Drivers will instantly read:

where the city is moving

without reading text.

This is exactly how:

air traffic control

logistics command centers

military radar

stock heatmaps

communicate information.

2. Anchors Show the Cause

Zones show energy.

Anchors show why.

Example on the map:

glow zone → Opera district
anchor → Olympia

Driver understands instantly:

Olympia exit → taxi demand

So the mental model becomes:

anchor = cause
zone = effect

That builds trust in the system.

3. Mobile-Friendly Map Layout

On a phone screen you must limit complexity.

Ideal structure:

MAP (70%)
BOTTOM CARD (30%)

Driver taps a signal → card slides up.

Example card:

OLYMPIA
sortie 22:24
~1200 personnes

signal strong
4 min

NAVIGUER →

This pattern is used by:

Apple Maps

Uber

Google Maps

because it works perfectly on mobile.

4. Reduce Map Noise

One mistake mobility apps often make is too many icons.

Flow should limit visible anchors to maybe:

3–5 signals max

Anything more becomes visual noise.

If there are many signals, show only the strongest ones.

Example logic:

show top 5 opportunities

Drivers don’t need everything.

They need the best few options.

5. Color System (your minimal philosophy)

You already have a good instinct here.

Keep it simple:

black → environment
grey → neutral info
green → opportunity
yellow → forming
red → competition

Green should mean:

movement opportunity

not just decoration.

6. Radar Mode (final clarification)

Radar should not look like Live.

Radar should look like a signal list or scanner.

Example:

OLYMPIA
exit 22:24

CHATELET
exit 22:45

ACCOR ARENA
exit 22:54

GARE MONTPARNASSE
train 22:40

Tap → map centers.

Simple.

Radar is basically:

city scanning
7. Final Navigation (the correct one)

Your proposal is the right structure:

CARTE
LIVE
RADAR
SEMAINE

Meaning:

CARTE → spatial awareness
LIVE → recommended action
RADAR → discover signals
SEMAINE → planning

That flow matches driver cognition.

8. The Product Is Now Clear

If we zoom out, Flow becomes:

MAP     → where energy is
LIVE    → what to do
RADAR   → what’s forming
SEMAINE → what’s coming

That’s a coherent system.

9. What Matters Most Now

Not more features.

Focus on:

signal accuracy
map clarity
mobile usability
driver trust

If the signals are right, drivers will forgive everything else.

10. My Final Design Advice

Flow should feel like:

a quiet expert guiding you through the city

not

a loud dashboard full of data

Calm interface → high trust.

And your current direction (minimal black + green) is actually very good for that.

navigation structure

radar concept
